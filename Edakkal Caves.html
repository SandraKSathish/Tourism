Edakkal Caves

Edakkal caves are well known for its pictorial depictions (cave art), which are thought to be of 6000 B C. To achieve the hollows, one needs to trek through the Ambukutty Mala. It will take around 45 minutes to climb the hill and you will never get baffled in your outing to these historical caves & it is one of the famous places to see in Wayanad.

Inside the cave, you can see two chambers. The lower chamber is 18 ft long, 12 ft wide and 10 ft high and the upper chamber is 96 ft long, 22 ft wide and 18 ft high. You can see animal figures and objects used by humans, on the walls of the caves.

These carvings give incredible confirmation for a very enlightened society who lived in the pre-noteworthy age. Edakkal caves have drawn awesome consideration of archaeologists and students of history around the world.

Location: Edakkal Caves are located at Wayanad, Nenmeni, Kerala 673595

Timings: Opens from 8:00 AM to 4:00 PM

Distance from Bangalore: Eddakal Caves are located at a distance of 273.5 km.

Distance from Bangalore: 261.6 kilometers.


Twenty five kilometers away from Kalpetta lays two caves which showcases nature’s skill and philosophy on architecture. These caves are located 1,200 m above the sea level on Ambukutty Mala. The name ‘Edakkal’ literally means ‘a stone in between’. Here you can see a cave formed by a heavy boulder straddling a fissure in the rock.

Many legends are there behind the formation of Edakkal caves. One of the stories is that, these caves are said to be formed with the arrows fired by Lava and Kusha, the sons of Lord Sri Rama. Another one is associated with Kutti Chathan and the Goddess Mudiampilly. The local people used to have a pilgrimage trip to this place to honour the Goddess.


The caves were discovered by Fred Fawcett, the then Superintendent of Police of the Malabar district in 1890, during his hunting trip to Wayanad. He discovered the Edakkal rock-shelter, situated on the western side of Edakkalmala. To his surprise, he identified the place as a habitat of Neolothic people.

Edakkal caves are famous for its pictorial paintings (cave paintings), which are considered to be of 6000 B C. To reach the caves, one has to trek through the Ambukutty Mala. It will take around 45 minutes to climb the hill and you will never get disappointed in your trip to these historic caves. Inside the cave, you can see two chambers. The lower chamber is 18 ft long, 12 ft wide and 10 ft high and the upper chamber is 96 ft long, 22 ft wide and 18 ft high. You can sight carvings of human, animal figures and objects used by humans, on the walls of the caves. These carvings give great evidence for a highly civilized society who lived in the pre-historic age. Edakkal caves have drawn great attention of archeologists and historians worldwide.

  • WHICH IS THE BEST TIME TO VISIT KERALA, INDIA?

    Tourist season starts from September and continues till April. May to August are off season, but during this period many travellers take advantage of the low prices offered by hotels across Kerala.

  • WHICH ARE THE BEST KERALA TOUR PACKAGES FOR FAMILIES?

    A 4-night 5-day trip is recommended for family tours. Members of family tours love to spend time in Kerala houseboats in Alleppey, Munnar tea gardens, boat safari and animal spotting at Thekkady and shopping at Lulu Mall in Cochin. You can also buy banana chips that the state is famous for, tea from Munnar, spices from Thekkady and Kerala sarees from handicraft stores.

  • WHICH ARE THE BEST KERALA TOUR PACKAGES WITH HOUSEBOAT?

    An overnight stay in a Kerala houseboat is recommended over just a day trip. A 1-night stay in an authentic houseboat with your loved ones will give you a chance to explore the beautiful backwaters of Kerala under the stars. A personal chef will treat you to local fish curry and Kerala meals right on the boat. Combine the houseboat stay with a trip to Munnar’s endless rows of tea gardens and at a nature resort in the heart of Thekkady.

    ♦ Munnar - A very popular hill station for honeymooners, nature lovers, tea tasters. Munnar has many mountains that look like a giant green carpet thanks to the beautiful tea gardens. Rajamalai, tea museum, the marayoor dolmens and a visit to a tea factory are some of the interesting activities to do in Munnar
    ♦ Cochin - Fort Cochin, Jewish Synagogue, Mattancherry Palace, St Francis church, Chinese fishing nets
    ♦ Wayanad - The green destination famous for its natural beauty and wildlife.
    ♦ Kannur - Expansive and serene beaches
    ♦ Bekal - Fort and shallow beaches.
